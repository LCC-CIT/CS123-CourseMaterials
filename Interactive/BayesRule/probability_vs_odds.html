Bayes Classifier: Probability vs. Odds

Detailed Explanation:

In everyday language, "probability" and "odds" are often used interchangeably, but in mathematics and statistics, they have distinct meanings.

  • Probability is a way of expressing the chance of something happening as a fraction or a percentage. It is calculated by dividing the number of successful outcomes by the total number of possible outcomes. For example, the probability of flipping a coin and getting heads is 1/2 or 50%. The value of a probability always falls between 0 and 1 (or 0% and 100%).
  • Odds are a ratio that compares the number of successful outcomes to the number of unsuccessful outcomes. For the same coin flip, the odds of getting heads are 1:1 (one successful outcome for every one unsuccessful outcome). Unlike probability, odds can be any positive number. The key difference is the denominator: probability uses the total number of outcomes, while odds use the number of other outcomes.

Comprehension Question:

If the probability of it raining tomorrow is 2/5, what are the odds of it raining?
