Bayes Classifier: Likelihood Ratio (Simpler Explanation)

The likelihood ratio is the new information you just found. It's a number that tells you how important the new clue is. If the number is big, it makes your final guess much more likely. If the number is small, it makes it less likely. It's the "magic number" that updates your first guess.
