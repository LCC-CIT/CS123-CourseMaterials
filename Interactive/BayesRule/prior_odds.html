Bayes Classifier: Prior Odds

Detailed Explanation:

Prior odds represent your initial assessment or belief about a hypothesis before any new evidence is considered. They are a starting point for the classification process. You calculate prior odds based on historical data or past observations. For example, if you are building a spam filter and you know from your old emails that about 80% are not spam and 20% are spam, your prior odds for a new email being "not spam" would be 80:20, or 4:1. These prior odds are crucial because they ground the classifier's decisions in a realistic understanding of the world. They give the system a baseline to work from before it even looks at the content of a new email.
